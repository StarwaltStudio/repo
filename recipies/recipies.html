Bone-Apple Teeth! Welcome to my Recipies

Enjoy Just-Deserts

It took me 5 years to make these cookies perfect, and maluable for sweet SWEET custimazation. After the refridgeration process is done you can get really creative with it ladies and gents let me tell ya.

Indulge in the ultimate homemade treat with this creative twist on the classic chocolate chip cookie. This recipe lets you shape soft, buttery cookie dough into adorable pinch-pot bowls, bake and enjoy with everyone. Perfect for holding your favorite sweet fillings whether you prefer gooey caramel, creamy Peanut Butter, fruit jam, Nutella, or even Marshmellows, these cookies offer a customizable dessert experience that’s as fun to make as it is to eat. With rich chocolate chips folded into the dough and a golden-baked finish, these mini cookie cups turn snack time into a deliciously personal adventure.

SURPRISE

BOMB-A** Cookies!

      Equipment Needed

    • Measuring Cups
    • Measuring Spoons
    • Big Spoons
    • A Whisk
    • Plastic Wrap
    • Parchment Paper
    • Mixing Bowls
    • Cookie Sheet
    • Oven
    • Fridge/Freezer

      Ingredients

    • 2cups - - - All Purpose Flour
    • 1 1/2cups - Cake Flour
    • 1 1/2tsps - Salt
    • 2tsps - - - Cornstarch
    • 1/2tsp- - - Baking Soda
    • 1 1/2cups - Brown Sugar
    • 1/2cup- - - White Sugar
    • 1 1/2cups - Unsalted Butter (melted)
    • 3 Egg Yolks
    • 2 Whole Eggs
    • 2tsps - - - Vanilla Extract
    • 1 Pound - - Chocolate Chunks/Chips (unsweetened)

    Directions

  • Remember you wont be using the oven for a few hours; we'll be mixing, wrapping and preparing the dough in the directions First.
  • Prepare your utensils, ingrediants and mixing bowls. In the first bowl Sift & Whisk in your All Purpose Flour, the Cake Flour, Salt, Cornstarch and Baking Soda. Whisk together until blended and Set aside.
  • Next in a sperate bowl, mix together the Brown Sugar, White Sugar, 1 1/2 cups of melted Unsalted Butter (or 3 sticks of butter, make sure it's melted and not hot), 3 Egg Yolks, 2 Whole Eggs and Vanilla Extract. Mix until blended.
  • Gently shake in your dry mixture into the wet mixture 1/3 of the way at a time. This will get THICC, so I recommend using a big spoon (not a mixer) to blend it all together. Once it's all combined, fold in the Chocolate Chunks/Chips until evenly distributed.
  • Now you can either store the Cookie Dough in a sealed container in the fridge for 3-4hours, or you can wrap it in plastic wrap and store it in the freezer for 1-2 hours. This will make the dough easier to work with allowing it to stiffen up, afterwords this makes baking a lot more even throughout the cookie.
  • Once the dough is ready, preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a cookie sheet with parchment paper.
  • The cookie dough is now hard but maluable, and can be made into "pinch-pots". This paticular part of the process takes patients and finger-work; Form the dough into small golf-sized (2-3in diameter) bowls with a thin (1-2cm thick and 2in accross) dough lid. You should be able to easily fit 3 rows of 5 pinch-pot cookies per row.
  • At this stage you can fill the little pots with anything you'd like inside a cookie:
    • Dark Chocolate
    • Peanut Butter
    • Nutella
    • Marshmellow Fluff
    • Caramel- and the list goes on
  • Once you have filled the pinch-pots, seal the contents inside and roll the cookie-dough ball in your hands blending the seems in. Place them back on the cookie sheet and repeat. Bake in the oven for 10-15 minutes (depending on the filling). The cookies should be golden brown on the edges and slightly soft in the middle. Let them cool for 5 minutes before serving (allowing them to stiffen).
  • Enjoy your delicious Bomb-A Cookies!
